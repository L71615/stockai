StockAI Design System

Industrial-Utilitarian + Editorial · Restrained · For clarity in a noisy market

North Star: Clarity in a noisy market

Aesthetic Direction

Function-first, data-respectful. Not "stealth finance" neon — a restrained, trustworthy palette where color is rare and meaningful. The dashboard should feel like a well-designed financial report, not a trading floor.

Decoration level
Minimal
Typography and spacing do the work. The data IS the decoration.
Layout approach
Grid-disciplined
Strict columns for data; editorial asymmetry for AI insights.
Motion approach
Minimal-functional
Number changes (200ms opacity), panel expand (250ms ease-out). No decorative animation.

Color System

Restrained: 1 accent + neutrals. Color is rare and meaningful. The only vibrant colors are red/green for P&L (Chinese market convention).

Design Decisions

What's safe (category baseline), what's risky (where StockAI gets its own face), and why each choice was made.

SAFE CHOICES

1. Dark theme

Every serious trading and finance tool uses dark mode. Users expect it for data-dense screens. No reason to fight this convention.

2. Red/green P&L

Chinese market convention: red = rise, green = fall. Non-negotiable. But we add +/- symbols for colorblind accessibility (from design review D14).

3. Card-based KPI layout

Summary metrics at the top of any dashboard is what users scan first. The convention is established; the differentiation is in what metrics we choose to surface.

RISKS (where we get memorable)

1. Calm density

StockAI shows 10 holdings with context where competitors show 50 in a scrollable list. More whitespace, bigger numbers, more context per position. This serves "clarity in a noisy market" directly. Cost: power users may feel it's "empty" on first glance. Gain: every number on screen earned its place.

2. Editorial AI panels

AI insights rendered like magazine pull quotes — distinct background, generous leading, left-border accent. Not chatbot bubbles. This makes the AI feel like a thoughtful analyst. Cost: may feel inconsistent with the rest of the UI if overdone. Gain: the AI content is instantly distinguishable from raw data — the "aha moment" from the design review.
